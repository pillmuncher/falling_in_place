* The Book

** Title (Working)

/Falling Into Place: What Good Design Gives You for Free/

** Central Claim

Rightness in software design is coherence under extension.

A design is right when future requirements do not force reinterpretation
of its primitives. Extensibility that falls out is the symptom; coherence
under extension is the criterion. Designed-in extensibility — plugins,
hooks, escape hatches — is the tell that the design isn't right.

Diagnostic inversion: /when extensibility just falls out, that is an
indicator that the design is right./

** Form and Tone

Humorous, story-driven, koan-like where appropriate. Philosophical depth
without academic heaviness. Insight revealed gradually — the reader
internalizes before the vocabulary arrives.

Inspirational models: GEB (Hofstadter), the Zhuangzi, Head First series.

The structure of the book should enact its thesis:
projection → failure → insight → stabilization.

In another register: a Taiji training manual translated into software.
意 (Yì) — intent — precedes implementation. 鬆 (Sōng) — release, not
effort — the system yields to find the path of least resistance through
the problem.

** Proposed Structure (from plan.org)

*** Part I — Irritations

instability.

- The PyQt QLineEdit Koan (written out in this session and prior)
- The bankrupt company (concurrency fiasco)

*** Part II — Failed Solutions
Typical responses: add guards, add patterns, add abstraction layers, add
control, add more rules. Show how these increase fragility.

*** Part III — Structural Shift
Gradually uncover algebraic laws, closure properties, projection as the
real test. Introduce Goodman and Luhmann, but lightly, embedded in
narrative.

*** Part IV — The Methodless Method
No checklist. No dogma. A way of seeing systems:

- Find the topology of the problem space.
- Find the algebra and its laws.
- Implement the basic operators accordingly.
- Synthesize the system from those operators.
- Let extensibility fall out.

The method emerges as recognition, not prescription. It cannot be learned
by reading the book. It can only be cultivated.

** Format

Blog series, one chapter per post, readable as a book. Feedback arrives
chapter by chapter. The format enacts the thesis: coherence under
extension, emerging structure, no premature commitment.

** Language

* The Projects

** Hornet

A Prolog-like embedded DSL in Python ≥ 3.13. Horn clauses via expression
trees, resolved by a triple-barrelled continuation monad carrying three
continuations: =yes=, =no=, =prune=.

The =prune= channel corresponds to Prolog's cut. In Prolog, cut is a
procedural anomaly that breaks the declarative reading. In Hornet, it is
a first-class operator in the /control/ algebra — not embarrassing, not
extra-logical, but correctly situated. The system is non-distributive and
non-commutative as a consequence, by design.

Architecture:
- =hornet.terms= — term algebra (syntax/algebra layer)
- =hornet.combinators= — triple-barrelled continuation monad (control layer)
- =hornet.clauses= — resolution engine and database (resolution layer)
- Bootstrap predicates

Key insight: =resolve()= is a functor from the term category into the
combinator category. The functor boundary means the resolution engine is
swappable without touching anything else. This was not a design decision
— it was the only honest representation of what was actually happening.

Affordances that fell out uninvited:
- Sagas through defunctionalization (reified combinators are serializable,
  enabling DDD-style sagas with compensation as alternative continuations)
- Temporary clause additions via ChainMap for situational / context-dependent
  reasoning, temporal rules, policy overlays, localized norms
- Modal operators (temporal, epistemic, deontic) layering cleanly because
  the ChainMap database was already the right shape
- FFI escape hatch to SWI-Prolog for performance: the free term algebra
  can generate actual Prolog code

When extension requires rewriting primitives, design failed. Hornet did
not require rewriting primitives.

** Pyraxial

An algebraic approach to axis-aligned rectangles.

Origin: OCR project, reconstructing sentences from screen captures by
computing bounding boxes for clusters of overlapping rectangles. A
colleague wrote a triple-nested for-loop with order-dependent results
(failing commutativity). The problem was stared at until its structure
became visible as an algebra.

First conjecture: Boolean algebra. Actual structure: complete
semi-distributive non-modular lattice with identity elements EMPTY
(for |, join/bounding-box) and PLANE (for &, meet/intersection).

The algebraic laws are not just documented — the implementation enforces
them. =inflate= and =deflate= reduce meet and join to four comparisons
with no control flow, making commutativity structurally guaranteed rather
than empirically checked.

The colleague's bug can now be diagnosed precisely: it failed commutativity.
Not "it had a bug" or "it was order-dependent" — but a specific algebraic
property was missing, which tells you immediately what class of fixes is
valid.

The abstract algebra vocabulary arrived /after/ the structure was already
seen. The name confirmed and sharpened what was already there.

** Causality

A minimal event-sourced ERP framework in Python, currently under
development. Implements CQRS and event sourcing with a clean separation
of commands, events, and projections.

Entities are projected from event logs via fold:
=entity := fold_left(transform, init(), events)=

The framework is the dogfooding vehicle for Hornet as a business rule
engine. The ERP domain is ideal for Hornet: complex conditional reasoning,
exception handling, jurisdiction-specific variations. Events are facts
added to the database; rules fire against them; the log is ground truth.

This also connects to the plan.org open question: Hornet deliberately
omits assert/retract. State change lives in the event sourcing layer.
This is not an omission — it is a clean separation of concerns that
maps naturally onto event sourcing's append-only log.

The Causality project demonstrates the book's thesis at system scale:
algebraic design of the event model, projection as the primary operation,
immutability enforcing idempotency, and extensibility (new event types,
new projections) falling out of the structure.

* Key Philosophical Contributions

** Rightness in Software Design (Goodmanian)

Rightness in software design is coherence under extension. This is a
Goodmanian claim: rightness is internal to the domain, not externally
imposed. A design is right not because it satisfies an external standard
but because it remains stable under the pressure of real use.

Goodman's non-vicious circularity applies: good design encodes practices,
and those practices stabilize and validate the design. Reflective
equilibrium between implementation and use.

** Abstraction vs. Generalization

These are not the same thing and conflating them causes real damage.

- /Generalization/ expands scope — more instances of the same structure.
  It is horizontal.
- /Abstraction/ changes the analytic level — a different vantage point
  that reveals essential structure. It is vertical.

Generality often follows from good abstraction as a consequence. Pursuing
generality directly, before the right abstraction is found, produces
premature generalization — many cases covered badly instead of a few
cases understood well.

This is what Perlis means: "In programming, everything we do is a special
case of something more general — and often we know it too quickly." He
is diagnosing premature generalization specifically.

** The Cut as Sequential Control Algebra

Prolog's cut is presented as a procedural anomaly that breaks the
declarative reading. This framing is an artifact of Prolog's theoretical
commitments, not a fact about the cut itself.

In a sequential control algebra, the cut is not an anomaly — it is a
natural operator with well-defined algebraic properties. Non-commutativity
and non-distributivity are expected from a sequential algebra. Of course
order matters.

Prolog was trying to be two things at once — a logic system and a
sequential computation system — and the cut is where those two things
visibly conflict. Hornet doesn't try to be both. It knows what it is,
so the cut fits.

The cut should have been called "commit" in Prolog. "Cut" is borrowed
prestige from Gentzen's sequent calculus (where cut-elimination is a
central theorem about consistency) applied to an operator with entirely
different semantics. A Goodmanian smuggling: the name imports an
association from the wrong world.

Connection to linear logic (Girard, 1987): linear logic treats facts as
resources that get consumed rather than freely reused. This maps naturally
onto stateful, sequential computation. Hornet's control algebra may be a
fragment of linear logic — an open question worth investigating. If so,
the cut's status there is very different from its status in classical
resolution.

Kowalski (1979): "Algorithm = Logic + Control." Prolog blurred this
seam by making control a second-class citizen dressed in logical clothing.
Hornet takes Kowalski seriously: logic and control are both first-class,
each in its own algebra.

** Rightness Smuggling (Goodmanian Pathology)

Philosophical problems — and software design problems — often arise when
a rightness criterion from one world is imported into another world
without justification.

Examples:
- "Moral truth": the rightness of "being true" (epistemic world) smuggled
  into moral discourse, producing moral realism
- Prolog's "cut": logical-world rightness criteria applied to a
  control-world operator, producing decades of confused theorizing
- "Designed-in extensibility": the rightness criterion of "generality"
  (mathematical world) or "flexibility" (product management world)
  imported before the design has earned it, producing plugins and hooks
  instead of affordances

The Zhuangzi Rejection: refusing imported rightness criteria rather than
arguing within them. Not a refutation — a declination. Huizi's
epistemology is not wrong; it is simply not the relevant world (River
Hao story).

** Specification vs. Implementation

The industry has conflated two distinct problems:

1. The /specification problem/: what should the system do?
2. The /implementation problem/: given a specification, produce code.

All tooling addresses the second. Vibe coding and Ralph Loops amplify it.
The specification problem is where most project failures originate and
essentially no tooling addresses it directly.

Algebraic design is a partial solution to the specification problem:
finding the right algebra is identifying what code should exist and
simultaneously what shouldn't, because illegal states become
unrepresentable.

** Lisa Simpson Loops

A principled alternative to vibe coding (Ralph Wiggum Loops: generate
code, check against tests, repeat until tests pass).

Lisa Simpson Loops: generate code, check against /formal specifications/,
feed failure back to the LLM, repeat until specs are satisfied. The
algebraic properties are checkable artifacts that anchor the workflow.
A commutativity failure has a name and a location — not "fix the bug"
but "this implementation fails commutativity; revise it so that property
holds."

Requires HITL at two points: when generating the formal specs, and when
deciding the loop is finished.

* Intellectual Tools

(See =tools.org= for the full file. Summary here.)

** Perceptual
- /Topological immersion/: loading a problem until its structure becomes
  visible as topology, not symbol or geometry
- /Sleeping on it/: after sufficient immersion, the unconscious ranges
  over the loaded structure without deliberate stepping; documented by
  Poincaré and Hadamard

** Reframing
- /What if it were different?/ (Wittgenstein): prevents the current
  framing from hardening prematurely
- /The Zhuangzi Rejection/: declining the frame rather than arguing
  within it
- /Restoring context-dependency/: "which world are we in?" before
  applying any rightness criterion

** Algebraic
- /Trusting pre-verbal intuition/: the structure can be seen before it
  can be named; the name arrives later and confirms what was already there
- /Retroactive vocabulary/: learning the formal vocabulary for something
  already done intuitively; load-bearing, makes certain bugs visible as
  bugs

** Diagnostic
- /Wart vs. scar/: a wart is unexplained; a scar tells you exactly what
  happened and why; the right abstraction locates anomalies in their
  right world
- /Detecting smuggling/: noticing when a rightness criterion has crossed
  a world boundary uninvited

** Design
- /Abstraction vs. generalization/: vertical (analytic level) vs.
  horizontal (scope); generality as consequence of abstraction, not goal

* Algebraic Properties and Their Software Affordances

| Property       | Affords                                              |
|----------------+------------------------------------------------------|
| Identity       | Null object, default value                           |
| Associativity  | Grouping independence                                |
| Commutativity  | Order independence                                   |
| Distributivity | Splits and joins (concurrency, parallel execution)   |
| Symmetry       | Reversibility — undo/redo                            |
| Idempotency    | Safe repetition — retries become harmless            |
| Closure        | No special cases — illegal states unrepresentable    |
