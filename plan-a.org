* The Algebraic Laws as Diagnostic Checklist

Revealed gradually through examples, never prescribed upfront.

** Identity
What does "nothing" mean in this domain?
- Null object, default value, the empty event log
- Absence of a natural identity is itself information — the operation is
  partial and boundaries need explicit handling

** Closure
No special cases — illegal states become unrepresentable.
- 意 (Yì) as ontological commitment: the algebra embodies intent about
  what the system should be able to mean
- Not defensive programming against bad states but defining a world in
  which those states don't exist
- Caveat: doesn't help when a cosmic ray flips a bit — this is a matter
  of intent, not magic

** Associativity
Grouping independence.
- What makes ~seq~ and ~amb~ work without parentheses mattering
- Event log replay order-independence within a transaction boundary

** Commutativity
Order independence — interesting where it's /absent/.
- ~seq~ is not commutative and shouldn't be
- The asymmetry is load-bearing; noticing where commutativity fails tells
  you where order matters and why

** Distributivity
Splits and joins.
- The MapReduce modal extension: query distributes over database layers
- The quantifier over parallel ChainMap stacks — ∀, ∃, ¬∃ from the fold
  predicate without adding new logical primitives

** Symmetry
Reversibility of operations — undo/redo.
- Event sourcing gives this at the data layer
- ~call_ec~ gives scoped reversibility at the computation layer
- ChainMap push/pop gives reversibility at the knowledge layer
- Consistent reversibility story at every level — what auditors need and
  what most systems conspicuously lack

* Philosophical Threads

Woven throughout, never foregrounded. Present in the examples before
they're named.

** Goodman
- Rightness criteria relative to symbol systems
- World-versions: the algebra doesn't describe the domain, it constitutes it
- Exemplification: the system doesn't just argue for its principles,
  it instantiates them

** Wittgenstein
- Meaning as use
- The limits of language as the limits of world
- The algebra's closure defines the world's boundary

** Zhuangzi
- Natural articulations: find the large gaps between bones and joints
- Cook Ding's knife — the skill that operates below conscious attention
- The Useless Oak — usefulness emerging from not optimizing for usefulness
- The happiness of fish — recognizing rightness by attunement, not proof
- Performative philosophy: the text enacts what it promotes

** Vervaeke
- Affordances as relational and participatory — not in the code alone,
  not in the programmer alone, but in the fit between them
- Relevance realization: the affordances are only visible to the attuned

** The Anti-Essentialist Turn
- Quine dissolving natural kinds
- Goodman demolishing essentialism
- Category theory: objects are residue of morphisms, not bearers of essence
- Davidson, Putnam: meaning is holistic and contextual

* The Historical Argument

- Early computing pioneers brought Platonic/Aristotelian assumptions invisibly
- "Find the objects" is Aristotelian essentialism operationalized as methodology
- The class hierarchy is the great chain of being in a text editor
- Even type theory drifted Platonic: types as ideal forms that terms inhabit
- Quine, Goodman, category theory arrived too late to correct the damage
- Relational databases, object hierarchies, canonical data models: all
  imposing a single ontology on domains that have none
- Your approach: programming methodology after the anti-essentialist turn
- Morphisms before objects. Algebra before taxonomy.

* The Taiji Manual Parallel

The book is a Taiji training manual translated into software development:

- Small number of principles generating entire practice through composition
- No special cases — just principles applied recursively to every situation
- The forms aren't a collection of techniques but a vocabulary for
  internalizing the algebra until it operates below conscious attention
- 意 (Yì): intent precedes movement; the algebra precedes implementation
- 鬆 (Sōng): release, not effort, not limpness — the system yields to find
  the path of least resistance through the problem

Pedagogical consequence: don't teach the principles first. Teach the form.
Let the body find the large gaps. The principles become visible through
practice, not before it.

* Chapter 17 of the Daodejing as UX Design Manifesto

#+BEGIN_QUOTE
Of the best ruler, the people only know he exists.
...
Of the work of one who is short with his words,
the hundred families say: We have done it ourselves.
#+END_QUOTE

- The trapping UI is the ruler they fear or abhor
- The good UI: you accomplish your task and think "I did it myself"
- The best infrastructure: the user thinks about the domain, not the machinery
- The best bass player: the audience doesn't consciously hear him
- The Christkönig acoustics: the voice fuller without the building announcing itself
- Making your best work invisible requires subordinating programmer ego to
  user experience — the hardest thing to teach

** When the Algebra Isn't Clean

- Not every problem space has a clean algebra
- Recognizing the topology of failures:
  - Where closure breaks
  - Where associativity fails
  - When you only have a semigroup, not a monoid
  - When you need a semigroupoid or partial monoid
- What it costs you when a law doesn't hold
- The difference between a design that fights the topology and one
  that respects it

This is not a failure of the methodology. It is its maturation.

The master doesn't always find clean gaps. Some oxen are harder than others.
The skill is knowing the difference.
