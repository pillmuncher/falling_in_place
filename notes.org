** Abstraction vs. Generalization
Generalization expands scope — more instances of the same structure.
Abstraction changes the analytic level — a different vantage point
on the same problem that reveals essential structure.

Generalization is horizontal. Abstraction is vertical.

Generality often follows from good abstraction as a consequence.
Pursuing generality directly, before the right abstraction is found,
produces premature generalization — many cases covered badly instead
of a few cases understood well.
